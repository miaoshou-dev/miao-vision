# The State Of Cloud Infrastructure In 2025

Source: https://example.com/cloud-infra-2025
Subtitle: Multi-cloud adoption, Kubernetes maturity, and FinOps discipline are reshaping infrastructure teams.

Global cloud infrastructure spending reached $340 billion in 2025, up from $270 billion in 2024. The top three providers — AWS (31% market share), Azure (24%), and GCP (12%) — continue to dominate, but alternative providers grew 47% year-over-year.

Kubernetes adoption hit 89% among enterprise respondents. Of those, 72% run Kubernetes in production, up from 58% two years ago. The average cluster count per organization is now 7.3, with larger enterprises running 25+ clusters.

- 340 billion USD global cloud spend in 2025.
- 31% AWS market share, 24% Azure, 12% GCP.
- 47% year-over-year growth for alternative providers.
- 89% enterprise Kubernetes adoption rate.
- 72% run Kubernetes in production.
- 7.3 average clusters per organization.
- 52% of cloud costs are now managed through FinOps teams.
- 38% average savings reported by mature FinOps programs.
- 26% of enterprises exceeded their cloud budget in 2025.
- 15% reduction in infrastructure carbon intensity year-over-year.

Container costs now represent 34% of total cloud spend, up from 22% in 2023. Teams that adopted FinOps practices reported 38% average savings, with the most mature programs saving 52%. However, 26% of enterprises still exceeded their cloud budget.

The shift towards sustainability is also accelerating. Infrastructure carbon intensity dropped 15% year-over-year, driven by more efficient hardware and better workload placement. Energy-efficient regions now host 41% of new cloud workloads.
